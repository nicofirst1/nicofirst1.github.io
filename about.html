About

Hello there!

You must be really bored to check this out, but since you're here let's give it a chance!

During my young age, I invested a major part of my free time playing games. I got lost in the fantasy world of Dungeon and Dragons where I matured my narrating skills and my open mind. Video games were of course part of the equation.

In them, I first encountered artificial intelligence in the way the NPCs were programmed and I grew a strong desire to understand them deeper.

During my studies as an engineer, I developed a strong relationship with the field of computer science and mathematical modeling. To me the concept of explaining and controlling the real world with man-made numbers and robots was a revelation, and it still is.

I advanced through my bachelor with dedication and enthusiasm, cutting out (a little too much) time to develop all kinds of projects spacing from home surveillance to simple sentiment analysis.

But studying is not my whole life, a major part of what I am I owe it to martial arts, and in particular Muay Thai which I practiced for over 5 years. It taught me to discipline myself in the moments of weakness and together with Krav Maga and Taekwondo , trained my body and mind for the past 10 years.

After an internship in the TRADR european project, where I learnt what team work really is, I undertook the master in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics at Sapienza University . The path was arduous but equally exciting.

During this period I learnt about Reinforcement Learning [RL] and set my mind to it. After an exchange program in the University of Groningen, where I further strengthen my english skills, I began my master thesis on the Mafia party game with Reinforcement Learning and graduated as an engineer in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics.

Right now I am undergoing my PhD on multi agent communication in RL environments.

I'm always open for discussion on any kind of argument both by mail or with a big mug of beer (possibly stout).
